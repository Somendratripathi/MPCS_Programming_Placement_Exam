Your program will take a list of ships and shots on a board, and will compute the final score of the game.

Input

The input contains the specification of a single game. The first line contains four positive integers: NN, MM, SS, RR (each separated by a single space). NN and MM specify the size of the board, as described earlier. SS is the number of ships on the board, and RR is the number of shots fired. You may assume the following bounds:

Next, there are S+RS+R lines, specifying the coordinates on the board of the ships and shots. The first SS lines specify the ships, and the remaining RR lines specify the shots. The coordinates of each ship/shot is specified by two non-negative integers (the x and y coordinates) separated by a single space.

Output

You must print HH/SS ships sunk. Score: PP points, where HH is the number of ships sunk, SS is the total number of ships, and PP is the final score.
